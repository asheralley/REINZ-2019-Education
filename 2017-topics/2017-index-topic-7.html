Question 1

Please answer in your downloaded booklet

1. What did the Tribunal, in Maserow v Real Estate Agents Authority (CAC 404) [2016] NZREADT 19 (24 February 2016), say in relation to obligations laid down by section 36(2A) of the Lawyers and Conveyancers Act 2006?

Question 1

Answer reference:

The Tribunal clarified that section 36(2A) of the Lawyers and Conveyancers Act 2006 prescribes the minimum supervision for the most competent agent, and that a branch manager must determine what level of supervision is actually required for each licensee. This possibly changing with each property.

Question 2

Please answer in your downloaded booklet

What did the Tribunal, in Maserow v Real Estate Agents Authority (CAC 404) [2016] NZREADT 19 (24 February 2016), say supervision must ‘be’ and ‘include’?

Question 2

Answer reference:

‘Supervision must be actual, it must be tailored to the circumstances of the agent and the property being sold, it must involve active involvement by the branch manager with the agent(s), including a knowledge and understanding of the issues with each of the properties being sold by the agency, if any. It should include an assessment of the competence of an agent to draft an agreement in English.

Question 3

Please answer in your downloaded booklet

What did the Tribunal, in Maserow v Real Estate Agents Authority (CAC 404) [2016] NZREADT 19 (24 February 2016), say the branch manager in the case should have done regarding the problem with clause 19?

Question 3

Answer reference:

Regarding the problem with clause 19, the Tribunal said that while he identified it, he did not make further enquiries with the licensees to confirm the true nature of the clause and its intentions which was regrettable as even though the agreement was signed had he intervened, the problems that were later experienced, (with the purchaser discovering that the carport needed to be torn down), could have perhaps been dealt with at an earlier time and prior to settlement to allow for the involvement of the lawyers for the parties.

Question 4

Please answer in your downloaded booklet

In case C10557, the CAC found that the agency’s advice to present a verbal offer on behalf of the complainant with the intention of avoiding a multi-offer situation was not adequate and not fair to all parties. What process should the agency have ensured was followed in this case?

Question 4

Answer reference:

‘At the time a pending multi-offer became apparent to [to the listing licensee], Agency protocols should have been in place for [the licensee] to follow. Had there not been adequate protocols, or [the licensee] did not understand the required procedure, she should have consulted an agency supervisory agent who would necessarily have been available for advice and assistance.’

Question 5

Please answer in your downloaded booklet

In case C10557, what arrangement in place at the agency did the CAC say was ‘not effective’ and ‘misleading or potentially misleading to clients and customers’?

Question 5

Answer reference:

The supervision provided by the agency at that time was not effective and was compromised by the understanding [the licensee acting as branch manager] was the authorised branch manager (and expected to supervise other salespersons) when in fact she was not qualified to act in this capacity. ‘The natural implication to be taken from the title ‘branch manager’, is that the person holds a branch manager’s licence, and to have a branch manager who does not have such a licence is misleading or potentially misleading to clients and customers. Further, a branch manager without a branch manager’s licence is not able to provide the proper supervision and management to salespersons required by section 50 of the Act.

Question 6

Please answer in your downloaded booklet

What improvements to supervision practices / agency processes would you suggest that could have helped to avoid the complaint that arose in this situation? Describe at least four improvements

Question 6

Answer reference:

- Systems in place to ensure communication is professional, appropriate and confidential.
- The agency should not have considered it acceptable practice to take a verbal assurance of a contact person to represent all parties, particularly in a ‘tenants in common’ situation, and to then expect that contact person would assume responsibility to pass on all relevant information and obtain all necessary signatures from other parties. There should have been direct contact from the listing licensee and a checking system in place at the agency.
- Processes should have been in place to prevent the issue of a verbal acceptance of the vendors’ counter offer being held for some 10 weeks by licensee 1 and then being presented to the vendors’ solicitor as a signed sale and purchase agreement without reference to all parties.
- A system should have been in place to check documents are properly completed prior to being processed.
- A system should have been in place to ensure copies of documents are properly provided to all parties.

Question 7

What improvements to supervision practices / agency processes would you suggest that could have helped to avoid the complaint that arose in this situation? Describe at least three improvements.

Question 7

Answer reference:

- As licensee X was engaged in his first auction and the agent licensee was aware he needed a more significant level of support than others in the sales team a more hands on approach should have been taken e.g. face to face meetings, progress reporting.
- Checks / follow up to ensure all prospective purchasers had signed the consent document acknowledging advice received about the non-consented exterior stair.
- Checks to ensure the exterior stair was excluded from the vendor warranties in the sale and purchase agreement.
- Checks to ensure the vendor was advised to include an exclusion or acknowledgement, or to take legal advice from his lawyer about the issue to avoid exposure of the vendor to the risk of being held to the warranty at clause 8.25 of the sale and purchase agreement.

Video 5

Question 8

Please answer in your downloaded booklet

What is described as ‘not enough’ to discharge supervision obligations under section 50?

Question 8

Answer reference:

Merely telling a salesperson not to act in a particular way or having agency policies which refer to specific conduct (without actual and meaningful regular supervision and training) is described as ‘not enough’ to discharge supervision obligations under section 50.

Question 9

Please answer in your downloaded booklet

What needs to be evident to show compliance with supervision obligations under section 50?

Question 9

Answer reference:

Evidence of actual and meaningful regular supervision and training / coaching.

Question 15

Please answer in your downloaded booklet

Give two examples of tasks that a line manager, rather than a licensed agent or branch manager, may carry out without compromising the supervisor responsibility.

Question 15

Answer could include two of the following:

For example: responsibility for ensuring that all salespersons working at the agency have a current licence, understand their statutory obligations, and meet their continuing legal education requirements are responsibilities that properly rest with a manager.

Question 16

Please answer in your downloaded booklet

Give four examples of what a supervisor being actively and regularly involved with a salespersons’ activities might entail.

Question 16

Answer should make reference to:

- Understanding the level of experience and skill of the salesperson, and placing conditions and/or restrictions on their real estate work as appropriate.
- Understanding the issues with each of the properties being listed and sold.
- Conducting regular meetings with salespersons being supervised, and asking questions to draw out matters that might be of concern.
- The supervisor making themselves available for questions, dealing with day-to-day issues, and fostering and encouraging communication.
- The supervisor ensuring that they keep up to date with industry issues, tribunal decisions, and guidelines issued by REAA so they can transfer knowledge and provide guidance to salespersons being supervised.

Question 17

Please answer in your downloaded booklet

Give three examples of factors that may affect a supervisor’s ability to exercise sufficient direction and control when supervising through ‘remote’ or ‘at distance’ supervision.

Question 17

Answer could include three of the following:

- the experience of both the salesperson and supervisor
- the availability of experienced salespeople to assist with the oversight of day to day activities
- whether the supervisor manages their own listings
- how many other salespersons the supervisor is responsible for
- the policies, systems and procedures in place at the agency.

Question 18

Please answer in your downloaded booklet

Give three examples of factors that may affect how many salespersons a supervisor can properly supervise.

Question 18

Answer could include three of the following:

- the supervisor’s capacity, capability and level of experience
- the competence of each salesperson being / to be supervised
- the supervisor’s judgement that they still have the ability to continue to meet active supervision obligation
- the supervisor’s judgement that they still have the ability to take the extra care required to supervised new and/or inexperienced salespeople
- The supervisor’s judgement that they still have the ability to exercise sufficient direction and control.
