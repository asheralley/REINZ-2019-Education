Question 20

Please answer in your downloaded booklet

What did the CAC say about licensee obligations in terms of due diligence when a customer has been advised to complete their own?

Question 20

Answer reference:

Answer should make reference to the following:
‘There appears to be a suggestion... that as a result of advising the complainant to undertake his own due diligence with the council that the licensee believed that he was therefore released from the obligation to pass on information to the complainant from that source. The Committee does not accept (this)... The licensee should have undertaken the due diligence with the council... Licensees are not entitled to expect customers to have undertaken due diligence, or to make presumptions as to what has been discovered as a result.'

In error, the licensees decided not to pass on information that was material to the purchaser in making their decision to purchase the business. What conflict of interest did the licensees say they faced which influenced the decision they made?

Question 21

Answer reference:

Answer should make reference to:
'They said passing on the information would have been imprudent and scandalous. They said that while they were mindful of the Code of Conduct, rule 6.4 and their obligations to the customer, they were also aware of their fiduciary obligations towards their client and they believed that disclosing such information would have been contrary to their client’s interest. They also said that as they had only received the information the day after the contract became unconditional, they did not think it would be helpful to pass on the information at that stage of the transaction.'

Question 23

Please answer in your downloaded booklet

What did the Disciplinary Tribunal say about when and how information about defects must be communicated?

Question 23

Answer reference:

Answer should make reference to the following:
'The Disciplinary Tribunal stated that this complaint illustrated the importance of making sure that best practice is that this evidence is put in writing and that the onus is on the licensee to do so.
It is our view that the Act requires openness. A licensee must be open from the beginning of the sale process and as soon as any defect is known. We consider that this means that the information relating to known defects should be available at every open home in writing. If defects are later discovered, then those defects must be notified in writing immediately to all known potential purchasers and in writing to any subsequent purchaser.’
