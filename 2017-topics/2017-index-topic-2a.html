What should the licensee have done with regard to confirming the legal tenure of the property?

Question 1

Answer reference:

Answer should make reference to the following:
The licensee should have reviewed the Property Guru document carefully instead of making assumptions, especially as there were obvious discrepancies. She should have obtained a copy of the certificate of title and checked it.

Question 2

Please answer in your downloaded booklet

What ‘red flags’ should the licensee have picked up on which could have avoided the error made?

Question 2

Answer reference:

Answer should make reference to the following:
The legal description the licensee wrote on the agency agreement should have given a clue to a competent licensee that the property was not a cross-lease. There was no flat number or separate DP number and the land area was not a share in the 962m2. Note: As the licensee did genuinely believe the property was a cross-lease, then a flats plan and title were even more necessary to check for unauthorised structures and exclusive use areas etc.

Question 3

Please answer in your downloaded booklet

What should the licensees have done in this case to avoid the mistakes made and the complaint arising?

Question 3

Answer reference:

Answer should make reference to the following:
They should have completed a title search, or had some other competent person search it. They should have ensured they were familiar with the information gained in the search and the original mistake of ‘limited to parcels’ being omitted in the original draft version of the sale and purchase agreement could have been avoided. At least, they should have ensured that the later edit was properly brought to the attention of the purchaser.
